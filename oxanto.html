01 - Problem Statement

The Problem

Patients in rural and semi-urban India lack consistent access to affordable, quality-assured pharmaceutical products. Existing brands are either too expensive for low-income households or unavailable through local pharmacies. Oxanto had zero product management infrastructure to bring multiple products to market simultaneously.

🏥
Distribution Gap

Rural pharmacies lacked reliable access to essential medicines from quality-certified manufacturers.

💊
Affordability Gap

Branded pharma was priced out of reach for low-income patients across non-metro India.

⚙️
Operations Gap

No PRDs, no QMS, no Agile process. Oxanto had zero PM infrastructure at founding.

07 - Key Learnings

What I Learned

Agile works outside tech. Clear sprint goals and accountability drove on-time delivery even with a non-technical team.
QMS is the highest-leverage PM tool in pharma. It eliminated duplicated effort, created accountability, and was the foundation for scaling.
PRDs are the connective tissue. Stakeholder alignment requires written documentation. Verbal agreements broke down; PRDs held teams together.
Phased launches reduce risk. Piloting in select geographies before full rollout saved resources and improved product-market fit.
