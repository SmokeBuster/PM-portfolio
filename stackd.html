The Problem
Students Leave Thousands on the Table
The average college student is eligible for hundreds of dollars in discounts, perks, and referral bonuses every year. Enterprise car rental fee waivers. Free Adobe Creative Cloud through university IT. Wells Fargo $325 checking bonuses. Spotify student plans. But 90% of these benefits go unclaimed — not because they don't exist, but because there's no intelligent system to surface them.
Existing solutions make it worse, not better. Honey applies codes blindly without verifying they work. RetailMeNot shows codes from 2022. UniDays promotes brands that pay for placement, not brands that actually benefit students. There's no platform that's genuinely on the student's side.

Key Product Insight

The most important AI decision was what NOT to use AI for. Voting, coin tracking, OAuth, and the floating widget are all rule-based — they don't need AI and would be slower and more expensive if they used it. AI is reserved for the tasks where judgment and real-world knowledge are actually required: ranking, explaining, discovering, and searching. This distinction is what makes the product fast, affordable, and reliable at scale.

Key Design Decision
Self-Cleaning Codes: The Killer Feature
The single most important product insight behind Stackd: the problem isn't finding coupon codes — it's trusting that they still work.
Honey applies every code in sequence and keeps ones that work. That's brute force, not intelligence. RetailMeNot shows codes with no recency signal. The result: users waste time copying codes that expired in 2023.
Self-Cleaning Codes inverts the model. Instead of the platform verifying codes (impossible at scale), the community verifies them through normal use. The 30-second delayed prompt after copying feels natural — not like a survey. Votes are tracked per user to prevent gaming. Three failures auto-remove the code. Ten successes award a Verified badge.
"The platform gets smarter every time someone copies a code — whether it works or not."
The compounding loop behind Self-Cleaning Code Intelligence
This is also the hardest feature to copy. Honey can't add community voting without breaking its auto-apply model. UniDays can't add negative feedback without exposing that sponsored codes don't always work. Stackd's self-cleaning system is both a UX feature and a competitive moat.

The backend runs on Render's free tier with PostgreSQL 18. The extension uses Manifest v3 with a service worker for background tasks and a content script for the floating widget. All AI calls are server-side — the extension never touches the Anthropic API directly, keeping the API key secure.
Architecture Decision: Why Google OAuth instead of .edu email

The original PRD specified .edu email verification for student authentication. I switched to Google OAuth for three reasons: zero friction (one click vs. email verification flow), inclusivity (international students often don't have .edu emails), and reliability (.edu verification requires a third-party service that adds cost and failure points). The tradeoff is losing guaranteed student verification — mitigated by the university personalization system which requires students to self-identify their school.
