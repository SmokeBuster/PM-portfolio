01 - Problem Statement

The Problem

Customers, typically office employees in Tier 1 cities with high order frequency, forget to leave a review after delivery. Company reports show a decline in YoY growth rate of monthly transacting users, indicating potential improvement in user engagement.

Nudging users to review would lead to higher user-generated content, increasing customer trust and retention, ultimately boosting average number of orders and revenue.

📉
Declining Engagement

App opening rate drops significantly after order delivery. Users don't return to the app to review once food is received.

🔕
Lack of Prompts

55% of users surveyed cited lack of prompts as the main reason for not reviewing - they simply forgot.

02 - User Research

03 - Proposed Solutions

How to Fix It

01
Push Notifications and Email Reminders

Send timely reminders through existing push notification and email infrastructure within a 24-hour target window after order delivery.

Top Priority
02
In-App Prompts

Whenever a user opens the app, prompt them about reviewing their last order through contextually placed banners and pop-ups.

Top Priority
03
Review Prompts in Receipts

Add review option in the invoice and delivery receipt, as users are more likely to open these to verify their order post-delivery.

High Priority
04
Streak Points and Leaderboard

Gamify the review process by giving users points and maintaining streaks. Create a public leaderboard for community experience.

Medium Priority
05
Highlight the Impact of Reviews

Remind users of the importance of their contribution to foster a sense of responsibility and community.

Medium Priority

06 - Key Learnings

What I Learned

User research validates everything. The survey data made the problem undeniable. 55% citing lack of prompts was the clear signal to act on notification strategy first.
RICE scoring removes subjectivity. Push notifications scored 96 vs in-app prompts at 78, largely because of lower effort. That data point drives prioritization clearly.
Notification fatigue is a real risk. The biggest trade-off: too many reminders could hurt long-term engagement. Throttling and user preferences are essential from day one.
